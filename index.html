Friederike Lais Krüger

I am a theoretical and computational physicist with a background in fundamental physics, covering particle physics, QFT, general relativity, cosmology, and atomic and molecular physics. In my PhD, I work on applying machine learning to reconstruct 3D protein structure from X-ray laser imaging data and on computational methods for structural biology and cancer radiation therapy.

AI & Society

2022
Master's thesis · CIAI, Universitat Pompeu Fabra · Supervisor: Jose Luis Martí
Should AI be incorporated into the governing of liberal democracies?

Argues that AI as an extension of collective intelligence can epistemically strengthen democratic decision-making, but only if built with human-centred, democratic values. Otherwise, governance power shifts to those controlling it. Examines questions of legitimacy, accountability, and epistemic autonomy.
